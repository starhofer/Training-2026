## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Conditional pass — with one clear, self-inflicted fail.**

**LLM assessment:** No generic AI-template tells found — no side-stripe borders, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no cream/sand/beige background, no uppercase tracked eyebrows, no hero-metric tile, no identical decorative card grid. The week numbers (01-18) are a real ordered sequence, not decorative 01/02/03 scaffolding. This reads as a handmade, domain-specific tool, not an AI template. The one real failure is internal: DESIGN.md names a "Solid-Fill Rule" as *the exact regression that triggered this redesign* ("Don't revert to pale tint chips/badges"), and the shipped code still uses pale-tint week-row and race-info-panel backgrounds with colored (non-white) text in dozens of places — the code contradicts its own design system's most emphatic rule.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` found 73 findings: 49 "color outside DESIGN.md" (mostly the same pale-tint literals and undocumented category colors the LLM review flagged independently — strong agreement between both tracks), 15 font-size and 6 radius values off the documented type/radius ramp, plus 3 document-level copy-style flags (43 em-dashes, 8 "aphoristic" constructions, the week-number sequence flagged as "numbered section markers").

**Likely false positives:** the week-number sequence (01-18) — these are real week counters in an actual ordered plan, not decorative eyebrow scaffolding, so this is exactly the kind of case the ban is meant to exempt. Several font-size/radius flags match documented per-component exceptions in the project's own sidecar `.impeccable/design.json` (e.g. `.badge-lg` 22px, `.ph h3` 18px, `.stype` 9.5px) rather than actual drift. The em-dash/aphoristic copy flags are more debatable — this is terse training-log coaching-note copy, a legitimate genre for short, punchy phrasing, not marketing copy; worth a glance but not treated here as true AI-slop.

## Overall Impression

This is a well-executed, handmade-feeling tool that mostly delivers on its own design system — solid identity colors, real monogram badges, genuine keyboard support on the primary interaction (week expand/collapse), and warm, specific coaching copy at the moments that matter most (taper, race day, post-ultra recovery). The biggest gap is that the color system has two unresolved jobs stacked on top of each other: identity (whose plan is this) and training-phase category (what kind of week is this) share the same four hues, and session-type tags borrow those same hues a third time — so "blue" ends up meaning Star/Xavier, Race week, *and* a Tuesday speed session, depending on where you're looking. That collision is what's dragging down consistency, recognition, and (via the missing dark-mode variants on every non-identity color) contrast in dark mode.

## What's Working

1. **Per-week coaching notes** tying every single week back to a named methodology (Roche/Koop/Uphill Athlete/Moehl) — real substance, not decoration, and the thing that makes the "why" legible without a separate doc.
2. **Progressive disclosure on week rows** — collapsed by default, and correctly keyboard-operable (`tabindex="0"`, `role="button"`, `aria-expanded` kept in sync via JS on Enter/Space and click) — unlike the sparkline, which got a partial version of the same fix.
3. **Milestone-anchored emotional copy** at the two highest-stakes moments (key race week, post-ultra recovery week) — matches PRODUCT.md's stated intent that energy should come from real milestones, not decoration.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Solid-Fill Rule violated at scale, contradicting DESIGN.md's own named principle.**
- **Why it matters:** DESIGN.md explicitly names this exact pattern ("pale tint chips/badges") as the regression that triggered the whole redesign, and explicitly bans it. It's still there — roughly 40 pale `.wh` row-tint and `.rb` panel-background instances (e.g. `background:oklch(0.94 0.045 232 / 55%)`), each paired with colored, non-white text — on every Race/Taper/Key-Race/Recovery/Team-Race week, across all four tabs. This is the single biggest gap between the code and its own documented standard.
- **Fix:** Convert `.wh` row emphasis and `.rb` panel backgrounds to solid-fill + white text (matching how `.wb` badges and `.trb` taper banners already correctly do it), or drop the colored tint in favor of a neutral surface highlight.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish`

**[P0] Session-type category colors are unpromoted literals that collide with identity/phase colors, and have no dark-mode variant.**
- **Why it matters:** "Hills" always renders in Angela's personal green, "Trail Long" always renders in Gary's personal ochre, and "Speed" itself flips between gray and blue within Angela's own tab from week to week — color has stopped reliably meaning anything. Because these ~7 category colors were never promoted to CSS custom properties (only the 4 identity colors got a `@media(prefers-color-scheme:dark)` override), they're also the reason dark mode has real WCAG failures: colored `.wp` labels (e.g. the Race-blue "Training Race:" text) compute to roughly 2.9:1 contrast against the near-black dark background — well under the 4.5:1 AA minimum — and the pale row-tints, tuned for a white page, composite into a distractingly bright patch against a near-black one.
- **Fix:** Give every category/session color its own custom property with an explicit dark-mode pair, distinct in value from the 4 identity colors; re-check contrast for every color-on-background combination once tokenized.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable colorize`

**[P1] The one field mobile users need most is the one most likely to truncate.**
- **Why it matters:** PRODUCT.md's stated use case is checking the plan on a phone, one-handed, after a run. The collapsed week row's fixed-width siblings (`wn`+`wdate`+`wb`+`wv`+`wc`+gaps+padding ≈ 262px) leave only ~100-115px for the actual week label (`.wp`, e.g. "KEY RACE: Georgina Marathon") before it ellipsis-truncates — on a 360-375px phone, that's exactly the content answering "what am I doing this week," cut off. No responsive rule adjusts this; the only breakpoint in the file is the 860px two-column layout switch.
- **Fix:** Add a narrow-viewport rule that shrinks/hides `wdate` or wraps `.wp` to two lines instead of truncating.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt`

**[P1] Sparkline "tap bar to jump" is keyboard-inoperable.**
- **Why it matters:** Each sparkline bar has `role="button"` but `tabindex="-1"`, so it never enters Tab order — the Enter/Space keydown handler wired for it is dead code for keyboard users. Announcing a control as a button that assistive tech can't actually reach is arguably worse than not marking it up at all.
- **Fix:** Change to `tabindex="0"` to match the week-row pattern, or drop the `role="button"` and treat the bars as a mouse/touch-only shortcut with a `title` tooltip only.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden`

**[P1] No visual marker for the current week.**
- **Why it matters:** PRODUCT.md states "what am I doing this week" matters as much as long-term trend — but the page gives the current week zero special treatment. Only past weeks get dimmed; there's no auto-scroll, no default-expanded row, no highlight, so every visit requires manually scanning dates to find "now."
- **Fix:** Extend the existing date-comparison script (already computing past/future per row) to also tag and visually mark or auto-scroll to the current week.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable layout`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (confused first-timer, one of the training partners who's never used this before):**
- "What am I doing this week" fails softly — there's no current-week marker, so Jordan has to date-compare every row against today.
- The legend implicitly teaches "this hue means this category" — a lesson that breaks the moment Jordan opens a Hills or Trail Long day and sees Angela's or Gary's personal color on an unrelated workout.
- If Jordan is Gary, or checks Gary's tab after week 12, the plan just stops with no explanation — reads as broken, not "season over."

**Casey (distracted mobile user, one-handed, post-run, in a hurry):**
- The P1 mobile-truncation issue lands squarely on Casey: the one field they need at a glance is squeezed to ~100px behind fixed-width chips, on the exact device this app is built for.
- Pale-tint race-week rows read at lower legibility (and dimmer still if also a past week), making "was this a race week" harder to spot at a glance than a solid chip would be.

## Minor Observations

- No `<h1>` anywhere in the document — heading hierarchy starts at `h2` (page title), then `h3` (runner names), then `h4` (footer) — a real if minor structural a11y gap.
- The new per-runner `.badge-glyph` decorative icon is correctly `aria-hidden="true"` and uses a `box-shadow` ring — technically a second shadow instance beyond the header-card gradient DESIGN.md calls its one deliberate exception, though defensible as a halo rather than elevation. Worth a second look since DESIGN.md chose monogram badges specifically to avoid an "illustrated mascot" feel, and this nudges back toward one.
- Race-calendar and coaching-note inline SVG icons don't carry `aria-hidden`, unlike the newer `.badge-glyph` icons — an inconsistently-applied fix rather than a missing one.
- The "Rest" session-type pill (`oklch(0.64 0.014 132)` background, white text) computes to ~3.35:1 contrast — fails the 4.5:1 needed for small bold text.
- Gary's program ends at Week 12 (and Angela's at Week 13) while the page subtitle still promises "17 weeks" — no closing/"season complete" state marks the shorter programs as intentionally finished rather than broken.
- Worth confirming with the author: Xavier's weekly-volume sparkline is value-for-value identical to Star's across all 18 weeks, and a marathon-pace interval session appears verbatim in Angela's plan despite her key race being a 50K with real elevation gain — possibly intentional shared programming, possibly an unswapped copy/paste template. Not asserted as a bug, but worth a quick check.
