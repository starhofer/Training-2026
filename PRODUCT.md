# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users
Star and their three training partners (Gary, Xavier, Angela), each following their own structured 2026 race-training program (marathon, ultras, key races). Casual, personal use — checked on a phone or desktop during or after a run, or when planning the week ahead.

## Product Purpose
A single shared view of the whole crew's 2026 training programs: what each person is doing this week, how their key races are lining up, and how training has progressed over the full 17-week block (Jun 8 – Oct 4). Weekly clarity and long-term progress tracking matter equally — the page should answer "what am I doing this week" and "how am I trending toward my key race" with the same amount of attention.

## Positioning
One shared view for the whole crew's races and weeks — everyone's plan, race calendar, and progress live in one place instead of four separate spreadsheets or apps.

## Brand Personality
Motivating and energetic, not clinical. It should feel like a good training log or a Strava screen — encouraging, a little exciting when milestones and key races come into view — while staying data-forward and easy to scan, never gamified or over-decorated.

## Anti-references
Not a corporate SaaS analytics dashboard — avoid gradient stat tiles, heavy card chrome, or generic BI-tool trappings.

## Design Principles
Weekly clarity and long-term progress are equally important; neither should crowd out the other. One shared surface, four personal views — the tab-per-runner switch should stay as low-friction as a glance, and never drift into a comparison or competition frame between runners. Data density without dashboard-itis: dense information (weeks, sessions, races) stays scannable without reaching for generic SaaS dashboard conventions. Energy comes from real milestones — key races, taper timing, trend lines — not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No specific accommodations required. Maintain solid contrast and continue supporting both light and dark modes, as the existing CSS already does.
